Roast Pork...Just Kidding!

For when your meat materials are sabotaged!

This innovative recipe recreates the taste of real roast pork using a variety of vegtables, spices, bacon, and other umami ingredients! Those who have eaten it have been shocked to learn that there is no pork loin!(The secret is to wrap it all in bacon).

Try it for yourself and see if you can tell the difference!

Ingredients

  • 6 white potatoes
  • 3 Eringi mushrooms
  • 1 large white onion OR 2 medium white onions
  • 1 sprig of rosemary
  • Butter
  • 2 packs of thick-cut bacon
  • 250ml of red wine
  • 60ml of sake
  • 30ml of soy sauce
  • 1 sprig of parsley for garnish

Cooking Instructions

  1. Peel the potatoes
  2. Slice the potatoes into quarters
  3. Chop the mushrooms and onions into equally-sized Brunoise (small 5-6cm cubes)
  4. Steam the potatoes until soft (about 15-20 minutes)
  5. Pan fry the onions and mushrooms in a tablespoon of butter until carmelized and golden brown
  6. Mash the potatoes
  7. Combine the onion and mushroom mix with the potato mash until the ingredients are distributed evenly in the bowl
  8. Pick the rosemary from the stem
  9. When the potato mixture is cooled, form it into the shape of a pork loin on the plate (log-shaped)
  10. Wrap roast in the bacon
  11. Tie the roast with twine, and intertwine the rosemary in between
  12. Bake in the oven at 375 degrees fahrenheit for 30-45 minutes, or until the bacon is golden brown. Remember to turn it halfway through the cooking process
  13. Boil the wine in a frying pan on high heat to reduce it
  14. Add a tablespoon of butter, the sake and the soy sauce to the pan
  15. Remove the roast from the oven, taking of the twine and rosemary
  16. Pour the sauce over the top
  17. Serve immediately
